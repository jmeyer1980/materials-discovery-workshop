## Response Scenarios and Strategies

### Scenario A: Positive Response (Best Case)

**Response**: "This looks interesting. I'd like to learn more."
**Strategy**:

1. **Immediate Reply** (within 2 hours):
   - Thank them for their interest
   - Propose 3 specific time slots for a 30-minute call
   - Attach your one-pager summary
   - Mention availability for questions

2. **Pre-Meeting Preparation**:
   - Send calendar invite with meeting link
   - Prepare technical demonstration
   - Research their recent publications for talking points
   - Prepare collaboration proposal document

3. **Meeting Follow-Up**:
   - Send thank-you email within 24 hours
   - Provide meeting summary and next steps
   - Share any additional materials discussed
   - Propose specific collaboration framework

### Scenario B: Interested but Busy

**Response**: "This is interesting, but I'm very busy right now. Can we connect in a few weeks?"
**Strategy**:

1. **Acknowledge and Accommodate**:
   - Express understanding of their schedule
   - Propose specific dates 3-4 weeks out
   - Offer to send additional information for review

2. **Maintain Engagement**:
   - Send relevant publications or updates in 2-3 weeks
   - Reference their recent work in follow-up
   - Keep communication light and informative

3. **Re-engage**:
   - Follow up 1 week before proposed meeting time
   - Confirm availability and interest
   - Update them on any new developments

### Scenario C: Polite Decline

**Response**: "Thank you for reaching out, but this doesn't align with our current research priorities."
**Strategy**:

1. **Professional Response**:
   - Thank them for their time and consideration
   - Express understanding of their decision
   - Leave door open for future collaboration

2. **Maintain Relationship**:
   - Add them to your professional network
   - Share relevant updates periodically (quarterly)
   - Reference their work in future communications

3. **Alternative Approaches**:
   - Ask if they know colleagues who might be interested
   - Inquire about future opportunities for collaboration
   - Request feedback on your approach

### Scenario D: No Response

**Response**: No reply after 5-7 business days
**Strategy**:

1. **Gentle Reminder** (Day 6-7):
   - Send brief follow-up email
   - Reference original email subject
   - Offer to provide additional information
   - Suggest alternative contact methods

2. **Second Follow-Up** (Day 12-14):
   - Send final follow-up with different subject line
   - Reference previous emails
   - Indicate you'll assume they're not interested if no response
   - Wish them well and sign off professionally

3. **Move On**:
   - Update your outreach tracking
   - Consider alternative contacts at UMich
   - Apply lessons learned to future outreach

## Meeting Scheduling Protocol

### Initial Meeting Request

**When**: After positive email response
**Duration**: 30 minutes (maximum)
**Format**: Video call (Zoom, Teams, or Google Meet)
**Preparation**:

- Send calendar invite with clear agenda
- Include meeting link and dial-in information
- Attach relevant documents (one-pager, technical summary)

### Meeting Agenda Template

1. **Introduction** (5 minutes)
   - Brief background and context
   - Overview of your materials discovery tool

2. **Live Demonstration** (15 minutes)
   - Real-time prediction generation
   - Export and integration capabilities
   - Specific applications for NOME Lab

3. **Collaboration Discussion** (8 minutes)
   - Potential collaboration models
   - Resource requirements and benefits
   - Next steps and timeline

4. **Q&A and Next Steps** (2 minutes)
   - Address questions and concerns
   - Confirm interest and timeline
   - Schedule follow-up if appropriate

### Post-Meeting Follow-Up

**Timing**: Within 24 hours
**Content**:

- Thank-you message
- Meeting summary with key points
- Next steps and action items
- Additional materials or clarifications

## Risk Mitigation and Professionalism

### Communication Risks

- **Over-communication**: Avoid appearing pushy or desperate
- **Under-communication**: Don't let too much time pass between contacts
- **Inappropriate Timing**: Respect busy periods (grant deadlines, conference seasons)
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Be aware of academic communication norms

### Professional Boundaries

- **Respect Time**: Keep communications concise and to the point
- **Maintain Professionalism**: Always communicate professionally
- **Handle Rejection Gracefully**: Accept "no" responses with grace
- **Preserve Relationships**: Even declined contacts can become valuable connections

### Documentation and Follow-Up

- **Track All Interactions**: Maintain detailed records of all communications
- **Set Reminders**: Use calendar reminders for follow-ups
- **Prepare Thoroughly**: Always be prepared for meetings and calls
- **Follow Through**: Deliver on all promises and commitments
